# The Record Is Free Forever

The reference runtime ships with a standing pledge, reproduced here because a governance convention is only as credible as the evidence layer beneath it:

> The tamper-evident audit record — its creation, its export, and its cryptographic verification — is **free on every plan, forever**. It will never sit behind a paywall, and its immutability guarantees will never be weakened. Trust in the record is the product; it is not for sale.

Precisely scoped: record creation on every tier, bulk and streaming export, hash-chain verification, signed decision proof tokens, offline verification bundles, and the appeal/contest paths are free. Retention *length* varies by plan per the published schedule; within the window, access is unconditional, and exported verification bundles remain independently verifiable offline, forever, without any account.

The pledge is enforced in the reference runtime's CI: a route-table invariant fails any change that puts a paid-plan gate on an audit, verification, appeal, or contest endpoint.

Full text: [gaas.to/record-free-forever](https://gaas.to/record-free-forever.html)

Other runtimes implementing this convention are invited to make the same pledge.
